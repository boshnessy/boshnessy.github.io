Web Developer

Actualize Coding Bootcamp & Apprenticeship

I started out my career in Web Development by completing an intensive, 3-month boot camp, where I learned Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, VueJS, and more. The great thing about Actualize's Bootcamp is that rather than learning a specific language or framework, I learned how to think as a software engineer and gained the ability to tackle any new technology.

For my capstone project, I built an app called ShareTheExperience that allows music fans to come together and share their concert experiences with others of similar musical taste. The app contains a forum for users to interact and share their photos, their stories, and their memories.

University of Missouri

School of Journalism

I was always a writer, but Mizzou's incredible Journalism School opened me up to the multitude of opportunities available to those interested in journalism. From regular, old-school journalism, to photojournalism, to public relations, the J-School had it all.

I ended up graduating with an empahsis in Strategic Communication and a vague interest in copywriting. While I still enjoy non-tech writing, I'm happy with where my rather unconventional path led me.
